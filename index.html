Cross Connection Control Unit

Ensuring Water Quality Everyday

Cross Connection Control Program is a Federal and State mandated regulation. Local municipalities must implement the Cross Connection Control Program, compliance and regulations varies from different cities and counties. It's extremely important to make sure you contact the right Cross Connection Control Unit to make sure you are following the regulations that's been adopted and enforced by the jurisdiction in which your Backflow Prevention device is located.

The main role of staff working in the Cross Connection Control Unit is to make sure customers are in compliance with what's expected of them. The Cross Connection Control Unit is a section that's usually associated with the Water Department. It is extremely important that customers know the different sections that exist within the Water Department because it will help speed up the assistance process when it is needed.

For the City of San Diego the Cross Connection Control Unit only have jurisdiction for Backflows located within the City's boundaries and not for the entire County of San Diego. For individuals who are new to the idea will find this information helpful the City of San Diego the Cross Connection Control Unit requires Backflow Prevention Devices be tested annually. Backflow Prevention Devices most often belong to Residential or Commerical buildings if there is a possibility of the distribution water getting contaminated or polluted a Backflow Preventer Device must be installed per City's standard drawings.

The City of San Diego Cross Connection Control Unit requires all Backflow Testers to become City approved testers. This means that any tester certified with AWWWA, ABPA, and ASSE must attend a one time tester orientation which takes place twice a year every January and July. To find out more information on how to become a City approved tester please visit the "Become a Tester" page. Customers, it is very important it for you to verify with us to see if a Backflow tester you've hired still holds a valid certification with our office. We strive to keep our list updated after every tester orientation. The list can also be found on the "Become a Tester" page.
